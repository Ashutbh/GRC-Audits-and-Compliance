# UK GDPR: Data Controllers vs. Data Processors

## Data Controller

* **Definition:** Controllers are the main decision-makers. They exercise overall control over the **purposes and means** of the processing of personal data. 
* **Identification:** If you decide *what* data to process and *why*, you are a controller.
* **Joint Controllers:** If two or more controllers jointly determine the purposes and means of processing the same personal data, they are joint controllers. (Note: They are *not* joint controllers if they process the same data for completely different purposes).
* **Compliance Burden:** Controllers shoulder the highest level of compliance responsibility. You must comply with, and demonstrate compliance with, all data protection principles and UK GDPR requirements.
* **Processor Liability:** Controllers are also responsible for the compliance of their processors.
* **Regulatory Action:** The Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) and individuals may take action against a controller regarding a breach of obligations.
* **Financial Obligation:** Controllers in the UK must pay the data protection fee unless they are explicitly exempt.

---

## Data Processor

* **Definition:** Processors act on behalf of, and **only on the instructions of**, the relevant controller.
* **Identification:** If you do not have any purpose of your own for processing the data and only act on a client's instructions, you are a processor. This remains true even if you make some technical decisions about *how* the data is processed.
* **Compliance Burden:** Processors do not have the same sweeping obligations as controllers under the UK GDPR and do not have to pay a data protection fee. 
* **Direct Obligations:** If you are a processor, you still have a specific number of direct obligations of your own under the law.
* **Regulatory Action:** Both the ICO and individuals may take direct action against a processor regarding a breach of those specific obligations.
